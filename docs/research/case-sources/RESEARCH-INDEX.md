**Estimation technique inventory** (the distinct methods to encode):
1. **Top-down population funnel** — known universe × successive % filters.
2. **Bottom-up unit × frequency × scale** — one micro-unit multiplied up.
3. **Segmentation / iterative subdivision** — homogeneous buckets, size each, sum (more segmentation = more accuracy).
4. **Percentage-bucket decomposition** — MECE % shares vs absolute counts.
5. **Supply-side vs demand-side framing** — size the same market two ways.
6. **Fermi decomposition / first-principles chaining** — errors partially cancel.
7. **Anchor-and-adjust with round numbers** — clean mental math (300M ≈ US pop, powers of 10).
8. **Sanity-check / ±25% reasonableness ranges** — defensible logic > precise figure.
9. **Assumption-stating-out-loud** — the *scored* behavior in PM mocks, not arithmetic.
10. **Scope-clarification before numbers** — geography/time/B2B-B2C/new-vs-replacement.
11. **Unit-economics / revenue build-up** — convert counts → money (units × price; users × ARPU).

*Cross-cutting:* consulting sizing rewards a clean MECE tree + disciplined arithmetic → single defensible number; PM estimation reuses the same machinery but is scored on assumption transparency, scope questions, and handling live pushback.

**Anatomy of a full interview** (the arc the engine must sequence + score, not just the case):
1. Greeting & rapport (1–3 min)
2. Intro — "tell me about yourself" / "walk me through your resume" (2–3 min)
3. Motivation / fit — "why consulting/IB/this firm" (3–5 min)
4. Behavioral / PEI deep-dive — consulting PEI probes ONE story 10–25 min across **Connection / Drive / Leadership / Growth** (McKinsey 2026 dims); IB/general = several shorter STAR answers
5. Technical block (IB only, 10–20 min) — DCF, LBO, accounting, EV vs equity value
6. Case / problem-solving (consulting) or product case (PM) — structure → analysis → math → recommendation
7. Guesstimate / estimation (can be standalone or embedded)
8. Candidate's questions (3–5 min) — scored as a prep/interest signal
9. Close — next steps

**Evaluation dimensions interviewers actually score on** (backbone of the automated rubric):
1. **Problem structuring / MECE** (~25–35%) — custom tailored tree vs force-fit framework.
2. **Hypothesis-driven thinking** — explicit early hypothesis prioritizes the dig; updates on contradicting data.
3. **Quant reasoning / math accuracy** (~20%) — method stated, accurate, narrated, sanity-checked, tied back. *Mental-math errors = #1 failure.*
4. **Business judgment / insight** (~15%) — non-obvious "so what", the case-changing question.
5. **Communication / signposting / synthesis** (~15%) — bottom-line-first, composed, clear recommendation + risks.
6. **Case leadership / initiative** — drives the case unprompted (passivity ≈ auto-fail).
7. **Poise / presence / coachability** — composure, rapport, takes feedback, "seems like a colleague".
8. **Creativity** (~10%) — original angles beyond templates.

**Scoring-engine takeaways:**
- Anchored **1–4 (McKinsey/CaseCoach) or 1–5 (CaseBasix) per dimension, scored independently** — never one averaged blob.
- **Pass bar ≈ 3 across all, with ≥1 "spike" at 4+**; a single hard-fail dimension can sink the verdict.
- Weight **structure + quant** highest; treat **math errors, no structure, no initiative** as high-severity flags.
- PM overlay: structured breakdown → user empathy/personas → metrics before solutioning → trade-offs → time mgmt → flexibility; behavioral = STAR with individual action + measurable result.
