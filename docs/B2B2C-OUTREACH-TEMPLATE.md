## What to demo (10-min outline)

1. **0-2 min:** start a session — "Book on China" or any estimation case. Show how Ash AI opens with the prompt.
2. **2-5 min:** walk through 3-4 turns. Highlight:
   - Push-back on memorized framework ("That's two words from a textbook")
   - Probe-end on every turn (no chatbot meandering)
   - "I don't have data on that — what would you assume?" (gating)
   - Per-turn citations: "Real EMs at Bain probe like this — see [link]"
3. **5-7 min:** end-session → debrief. Show the dimensional scoring (structure / quant / judgment / communication / composure) + ideal walkthrough.
4. **7-9 min:** show the dossier system. Ask a domain question ("what's the typical margin in book publishing?") — show the AI's grounded answer.
5. **9-10 min:** the ask: club-wide rollout terms.

## What success looks like

- **Week 1:** 3-5 DMs sent, 1 reply
- **Week 2:** 1 demo booked
- **Week 3:** 1 club agrees to a 6-week cohort pilot
- **Week 4:** 30+ active users from that club, real cohort signal data flowing into eval

If by Week 4 no club has bitten, pivot: drop B2B2C entirely, focus on individual user growth (LinkedIn organic content, Reddit r/consulting, IIM-A/B/C alumni groups).

## Why NOT to start with paid acquisition

- Casepad is on a zero-budget rule
- Paid ads on a non-monetized product = burning money
- B2B2C is the only legitimate distribution path that respects zero-budget AND gives real signal
- Organic LinkedIn (Ash posting his own learnings) is the only other zero-cost channel; combine both

## What to do if a club says "we already have RocketBlocks/LOMS"

Counter:
- "Those are great — and complementary. LOMS is video; CasePad is conversational practice. RocketBlocks is drills; we have those + a full chat interview. Most importantly, both are USD-priced and US-context. CasePad is INR-pricing-ready, Indian-context cases (we have ₹ amounts and SEBI references baked into every case dossier). Your students get more practice volume and better cohort data."
- Demo the dossier with Indian numbers. That's the differentiator they can't ignore.
