# Interviewer Playbook — Sections 10-12 (Rounds, Questions, Anti-Patterns)

> Compiled 2026-05-08 from web research. Findings count updated as compilation progresses.

---

## 10. Round-specific differences

### 10.1 First round (typically EM/Manager-level interviewer)

1. First-round interviews are typically conducted by **Project Leaders, Engagement Managers, Principals, or MBA/manager-level consultants** — not partners. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
2. Roughly **80% of the first-round interview is centered around a case**, with limited fit/behavioral. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
3. First rounds usually involve **2 interviews** (sometimes back-to-back), each with one case + brief behavioral. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
4. First-round cases are often **"off-the-shelf" and scripted** — interviewers follow a near-verbatim guide. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
5. Interviewers in round one are **screening for the question: "Is this candidate worth putting in front of partners for 2-3 more hours?"** [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
6. First-round cases tend to test **quantitative and structured problem-solving fundamentals** more heavily than fit. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
7. Interviewers are **more lenient in round one** — slip-ups in one element are forgiven if potential is shown elsewhere. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
8. McKinsey first-round cases are **interviewer-led**: the interviewer drives the case, asking specific sub-questions in sequence. [Source: McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)
9. BCG and Bain first-round cases tend to be **candidate-led**: candidate sets the structure, picks the branch to explore. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/how-different-are-the-interviews-at-mckinsey-bain-and-bcg/)
10. First-round interviewers expect a **standard framework upfront** even if it is not perfectly tailored — they want to see structured thinking baseline. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-case-interview)
11. Interviewers screen for **"the airplane test"** — would I want to be stuck on a 6-hour flight with this person? Even in round one. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
12. First-round case prompts are **shorter, often 25-35 minutes total** including fit. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
13. Round one tests **basic mental math under time pressure** — interviewer expects answers within ~60-90 seconds for typical calculations. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-guide)
14. First-round interviewers often **stick rigidly to the case script**, meaning candidates who go off-piste get pulled back. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
15. McKinsey first round focuses heavily on the **PSG (Problem Solving Game / Solve)** as a screen before live cases. [Source: McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)
16. First-round signal: **"Can this candidate execute the basics — structure, math, hypothesis — without coaching?"** [Source: MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview)
17. Bain first round emphasizes **"answer-first" communication** — top-down structure even in early questions. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)

### 10.2 Second round (Principal-level)

1. Second-round interviews are run by **Principals, Senior Managers, or Associate Partners** at BCG; **Senior EMs/Associate Principals** at McKinsey. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/i-was-interviewed-by-a-partner-in-first-round-case-interview-at-bcg-and-i-will-be-interviewed-by-a-project-leader-in-the-second-case-interview-is-it-common-many-people-said-that-we-would-be-interviewe-22122)
2. Second-round cases are **harder, sometimes with unfamiliar structures** designed to break templated frameworks. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
3. **45-60 minute interview**, split roughly evenly between case + fit. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
4. Interviewers expect candidates to be **more top-down, quicker, and more insightful** — less hand-holding. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/en/consulting-forum/difference-between-first-round-and-second-round-interviews-9)
5. Less interest in **calculation questions** in round two; more interest in **client engagement and judgment**. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/en/consulting-forum/difference-between-first-round-and-second-round-interviews-9)
6. Second round is often **multi-case format** at McKinsey — 2-3 back-to-back cases in one day. [Source: McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)
7. Principals deliberately **add curveballs mid-case** — e.g. new data dump, changed objective — to test adaptability. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)
8. Round two cases often draw from **the interviewer's actual project experience** — less scripted. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
9. Principals probe **"so what?"** harder — surfacing whether the candidate can extract implications, not just analyze. [Source: Caseinterview.com synthesis](https://caseinterview.com/synthesis-in-case-interviews)
10. Round two tests **client-readiness** — could you walk into a client meeting tomorrow and add value? [Source: MyConsultingOffer](https://www.myconsultingoffer.org/case-study-interview-prep/final-round/)
11. Cases at this level often have **ambiguous objectives** — candidate must clarify and pick the right metric. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)
12. Principals frequently push back with **"why not the opposite?"** to test conviction. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)
13. Expect deeper **brainstorming with quantitative grounding** — "give me 5 ideas, then size each one." [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/how-can-you-brainstorm-in-a-structured-way-during-a-case-study-interview-436)
14. Fit questions in round two probe **leadership under conflict, ambiguity, and failure** — not just achievement stories. [Source: Medium - Antoine](https://medium.com/@zerotombb/preparing-your-personal-experience-stories-for-mckinsey-bcg-and-bain-interviews-6b11395ac4e7)
15. Round two interviewers **take notes more aggressively** and reference earlier comments to test consistency. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)
16. Principal-level interviewers often deliver the case **without notes** — they know it cold and watch you, not the page. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
17. Second round assesses **"would I staff this person on my team next Monday?"** — a much higher bar than round one. [Source: MyConsultingOffer](https://www.myconsultingoffer.org/case-study-interview-prep/final-round/)

### 10.3 Partner round (storytelling + EQ test)

1. Partner round is run by **Partners and Senior Partners** (sometimes one per session, sometimes a panel). [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
2. Partners often **use the same case for years** — they deliver it without script, freeform. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
3. Partner cases are **less structured** — partners give candidates more room to lead. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
4. Partners spend **more time on fit/PEI** than on quant — they trust prior rounds verified case skills. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/how-different-are-the-interviews-at-mckinsey-bain-and-bcg/)
5. Partner round has **higher stakes per slot** — they decide the offer, not just advance the candidate. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)
6. Partners screen for **"would I sell this person to my client?"** — executive presence, gravitas, polish. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)
7. Storytelling is critical: PEI answers must show **arc, conflict, decision, and impact** — not chronological recap. [Source: Medium - Antoine](https://medium.com/@zerotombb/preparing-your-personal-experience-stories-for-mckinsey-bcg-and-bain-interviews-6b11395ac4e7)
8. McKinsey's PEI specifically tests **Personal Impact, Entrepreneurial Drive, and Inclusive Leadership** at partner round. [Source: McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)
9. Partners frequently interrupt to **dig into one detail for 5-10 minutes** — testing depth over breadth. [Source: Medium - Antoine](https://medium.com/@zerotombb/preparing-your-personal-experience-stories-for-mckinsey-bcg-and-bain-interviews-6b11395ac4e7)
10. Partners probe **EQ and self-awareness** through "tell me about a failure" and follow-ups: "What did you do differently next time? What would you still change?" [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-interview-questions)
11. The partner case often becomes a **dialogue, not a quiz** — partners want to see if you can hold a peer-level conversation. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
12. Partner round may include **"why our firm?"** with very specific follow-ups about practices, recent projects, partners' clients. [Source: Sensei AI](https://www.senseicopilot.com/blog/bain-vs-bcg-vs-mckinsey-interview-differences-explained)
13. Partners stress-test **commitment to consulting** — probing whether the candidate is using offer as backup. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)
14. Bain partner round emphasizes **cultural fit ("A Bainie never lets another Bainie fail")** — collaborative storytelling. [Source: Sensei AI](https://www.senseicopilot.com/blog/bain-vs-bcg-vs-mckinsey-interview-differences-explained)
15. BCG partner round emphasizes **intellectual depth and creativity** — partners often push on "what's the second-order effect?" [Source: Sensei AI](https://www.senseicopilot.com/blog/bain-vs-bcg-vs-mckinsey-interview-differences-explained)
16. McKinsey partner round emphasizes **structured top-down communication** even in the fit portion. [Source: McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)
17. Partners often ask **"what questions do you have for me?"** — and judge candidate by quality of questions (industry-specific > generic). [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-interview-questions)
18. Partners weight **the "hire / no-hire / strong yes" tier** more heavily — a soft yes from a partner often fails the candidate. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)

### 10.4 What changes across rounds (bar progression)

1. **Bar rises from "competent" to "client-ready" to "partner-track"** across rounds. [Source: MyConsultingOffer](https://www.myconsultingoffer.org/case-study-interview-prep/final-round/)
2. Case complexity increases: round 1 = clear prompt, round 2 = ambiguous prompt, partner = open-ended client problem. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)
3. Time pressure increases: principals expect faster synthesis; partners expect snap recommendations under data scarcity. [Source: PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/en/consulting-forum/difference-between-first-round-and-second-round-interviews-9)
4. Fit weight grows: ~10-20% in round 1 → 30-40% in round 2 → 50%+ at partner round. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
5. Quant rigor decreases as a screen but **errors are punished more heavily** — at partner level a single math mistake can sink the offer. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)
6. Structural rigidity decreases: round 1 wants a clean MECE tree; partner wants insight-led prioritization. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)
7. Communication standard shifts: from "clear and structured" → "executive presence" → "trusted advisor voice." [Source: MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview-provide-recommendations)
8. Curveballs increase in frequency and severity round-over-round. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)
9. PEI depth increases: surface story → detailed actions → second/third "why?" probes. [Source: Medium - Antoine](https://medium.com/@zerotombb/preparing-your-personal-experience-stories-for-mckinsey-bcg-and-bain-interviews-6b11395ac4e7)
10. Mental math expectation shifts: round 1 = arithmetic accuracy; round 2 = estimation savvy; partner = number sense + "smell test." [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-guide)
11. Hiring decision threshold tightens: a "yes" from one round-1 interviewer ≠ a "yes" from a partner. Partner-level disagreement often kills offers. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)
12. Round 2/partner cases are more likely to **draw from real client engagements** the interviewer led. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
13. Early rounds reward **process compliance** (clean steps); later rounds reward **judgment under ambiguity**. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)
14. Interview length grows: round 1 ~30 min, round 2 ~45 min, partner often 60-90 min including coffee chat. [Source: RocketBlocks](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/difference-between-first-and-second-round-consulting-interviews.php)
15. By partner round, the **"likeability" filter is fully active** — partners pick people they want around. [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/bcg-mckinsey-case-interviews-final-round)

## 11. Common interviewer questions (with what they're testing)

### 11.1 Opening / problem-statement probes

> **Q1:** "So, here's the situation. Our client is [X]. They're facing [Y]. How would you think about this?"
> **Tests:** opening structure instinct, listening, clarification reflex
> **Source:** [PrepLounge case structure](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)

> **Q2:** "Before we dive in, what's your initial reaction to this problem?"
> **Tests:** business intuition, hypothesis-led thinking
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)

> **Q3:** "Can you play back to me what you think the question is?"
> **Tests:** active listening, ability to restate the prompt cleanly
> **Source:** [PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)

> **Q4:** "What's the first thing you'd want to know?"
> **Tests:** prioritization, hypothesis-driven thinking
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-guide)

> **Q5:** "Our client's profits have dropped 20%. What's going on?"
> **Tests:** profitability framework, revenue/cost decomposition
> **Source:** [RocketBlocks Profitability](https://www.rocketblocks.me/blog/profitability-case-questions.php)

> **Q6:** "Our client is considering entering the [X] market. Should they?"
> **Tests:** market entry framework — should/can/how
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview Market Entry](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview)

> **Q7:** "Our client wants to grow revenue by 50% in 3 years. How would you advise them?"
> **Tests:** growth strategy, organic vs inorganic, ambition vs feasibility
> **Source:** [IGotAnOffer 47 cases](https://igotanoffer.com/blogs/mckinsey-case-interview-blog/case-interview-examples)

> **Q8:** "Take a moment to organize your thoughts. Let me know when you're ready."
> **Tests:** composure, structuring time discipline (typically 60-90 seconds)
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)

> **Q9:** "What do you think the client's real objective is here?"
> **Tests:** ability to look past the stated problem to the underlying business need
> **Source:** [MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview)

> **Q10:** "How big a problem is this for the client?"
> **Tests:** materiality sense, ability to size the issue
> **Source:** [PrepLounge profitability](https://www.preplounge.com/en/case-interview-basics/profitability-case)

> **Q11:** "Why do you think they're asking us [the consultants] this question now?"
> **Tests:** awareness of business context, urgency framing
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/synthesis-in-case-interviews)

> **Q12:** "What would success look like for the client?"
> **Tests:** objective clarification, KPI thinking
> **Source:** [Casebasix Market Entry](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview-guide)

> **Q13:** "Let's say you had only one piece of information you could ask for — what would it be?"
> **Tests:** prioritization, hypothesis sharpness
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)

> **Q14:** "How would you approach this problem?"
> **Tests:** structuring, MECE thinking, communication
> **Source:** [McKinsey Careers](https://www.mckinsey.com/careers/interviewing)

> **Q15:** "What kind of business is this and how do they make money?"
> **Tests:** business model literacy, value chain understanding
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)

> **Q16:** "What's your gut say before we dig into the data?"
> **Tests:** willingness to commit to a hypothesis early
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)

### 11.2 Clarification probes

> **Q17:** "What does the client mean by [ambiguous term in prompt]?"
> **Tests:** ability to define scope, not assume
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/are-there-any-useful-frameworkschecklists-for-asking-clarifying-questions-3190)

> **Q18:** "What's the client's primary objective — profit, revenue, market share, or something else?"
> **Tests:** objective clarification (BOTM framework)
> **Source:** [PrepLounge BOTM](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/are-there-any-useful-frameworkschecklists-for-asking-clarifying-questions-3190)

> **Q19:** "What's the time horizon — short-term fix or long-term strategy?"
> **Tests:** scope definition
> **Source:** [PrepLounge BOTM](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/are-there-any-useful-frameworkschecklists-for-asking-clarifying-questions-3190)

> **Q20:** "What geography are we operating in?"
> **Tests:** market scoping
> **Source:** [PrepLounge BOTM](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/are-there-any-useful-frameworkschecklists-for-asking-clarifying-questions-3190)

> **Q21:** "Is the company public, private, PE-owned? Any constraints on capital?"
> **Tests:** capital structure literacy
> **Source:** [Casebasix Profitability](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/profitability-case-interview-guide)

> **Q22:** "What products or services does the client offer?"
> **Tests:** business model clarification before structuring
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)

> **Q23:** "Has the client tried anything to address this already?"
> **Tests:** awareness of prior actions, learning from history
> **Source:** [MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview-profitability-analysis)

> **Q24:** "Why is the client considering this now?"
> **Tests:** trigger event awareness
> **Source:** [Casebasix Market Entry](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview-guide)

> **Q25:** "What does the competitive landscape look like?"
> **Tests:** awareness that competitive context shapes answer
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview Market Entry](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview)

> **Q26:** "What's the client's current market share and position?"
> **Tests:** baseline grounding before recommending change
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)

> **Q27:** "Are there any constraints I should know about — regulatory, capital, leadership?"
> **Tests:** awareness of feasibility filters
> **Source:** [Casebasix Market Entry](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview-guide)

> **Q28:** "Can I assume X for now and you'll correct me if it matters?"
> **Tests:** smart assumption-making vs question-spam
> **Source:** [PrepLounge clarifying questions](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-during-case-study-14947)

> **Q29:** "Is this a one-off problem or part of a broader trend in the industry?"
> **Tests:** macro/industry awareness
> **Source:** [Casebasix Profitability](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/profitability-case-interview-guide)

> **Q30:** "What's the client's risk appetite?"
> **Tests:** strategic posture clarification
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview Market Entry](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview)

> **Q31:** "Is there a specific success metric or KPI they care about?"
> **Tests:** sharpening the objective into a measurable target
> **Source:** [Casebasix Market Entry](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/market-entry-case-interview-guide)

> **Q32:** "What's the client's value chain — do they manufacture, distribute, retail, or all three?"
> **Tests:** operational literacy
> **Source:** [PrepLounge BOTM](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/are-there-any-useful-frameworkschecklists-for-asking-clarifying-questions-3190)

### 11.3 Structure probes

> **Q33:** "Walk me through your structure."
> **Tests:** top-down communication, MECE rigor
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)

> **Q34:** "Why these buckets and not others?"
> **Tests:** justification of MECE choice
> **Source:** [PrepLounge case structure](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)

> **Q35:** "Where would you start, and why?"
> **Tests:** prioritization within a framework
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)

> **Q36:** "What's missing from your structure?"
> **Tests:** self-awareness, ability to spot gaps under pressure
> **Source:** [MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview)

> **Q37:** "Is this MECE?"
> **Tests:** mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive discipline
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)

> **Q38:** "How does this structure tie back to the client's objective?"
> **Tests:** that the framework is custom, not templated
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview McKinsey](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-case-interview)

> **Q39:** "If you could only pursue one branch, which one and why?"
> **Tests:** prioritization with conviction
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)

> **Q40:** "Can you draw that out for me?"
> **Tests:** ability to visualize and communicate hierarchy
> **Source:** [PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)

> **Q41:** "What's your hypothesis under each bucket?"
> **Tests:** hypothesis-led structure, not just bucketing
> **Source:** [Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)

> **Q42:** "If I told you we already analyzed [bucket X] and it's not the issue — where would you go next?"
> **Tests:** flexibility, not framework-locked
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview MBB](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mbb-case-interview)

> **Q43:** "How is your framework different from a textbook profitability/market-entry framework?"
> **Tests:** customization to the case
> **Source:** [HackingTheCaseInterview McKinsey](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-case-interview)

> **Q44:** "What data would you need to validate each branch?"
> **Tests:** linking structure to data needs (analytical foresight)
> **Source:** [PrepLounge](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)

> **Q45:** "If you had 30 minutes total with the CEO, where would you spend most of your time?"
> **Tests:** prioritization under client time scarcity
> **Source:** [MyConsultingCoach](https://www.myconsultingcoach.com/case-interview-provide-recommendations)

## 12. Anti-patterns — what interviewers DON'T do

1. **Don't give the answer.** Interviewers are trained to redirect, not solve. If a candidate asks "is X correct?", a trained interviewer responds "what do *you* think?" [Source: Caseinterview.com](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
2. **Don't say "good job" mid-case.** Positive feedback distorts the candidate's path; trained interviewers stay neutral. [Source: Interview IQ poker face](https://interviewiq.com.au/how-do-you-deal-with-poker-faced-interviewers)
3. **Don't laugh at wrong answers.** Trained MBB interviewers protect candidate dignity — laughing biases the rest of the interview. [Source: Camden Kelly](https://camdenkelly.com/fired-before-youre-hired-five-ways-to-ruin-any-interview/)
4. **Don't course-correct prematurely.** Even if a candidate goes down a wrong branch, interviewers let them follow the logic to see if they self-correct. [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
5. **Don't lead with the framework hint.** Interviewers do not say "have you thought about a 4P framework?" — they wait for candidate to propose. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview McKinsey](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-case-interview)
6. **Don't react visibly to bad math.** A wince or sigh tells the candidate they're wrong before they can self-check. [Source: Interview IQ poker face](https://interviewiq.com.au/how-do-you-deal-with-poker-faced-interviewers)
7. **Don't volunteer data the candidate didn't ask for.** Information is given on request — testing whether the candidate knows what to ask. [Source: PrepLounge clarifying](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)
8. **Don't break character to chitchat mid-case.** Disrupts the candidate's flow and biases time. [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
9. **Don't argue with a correct answer just to win.** Pushback is a probe; if the candidate defends well, the interviewer accepts and moves on. [Source: Road to Offer pushback](https://www.roadtooffer.com/blog/case-interview-pushback-challenge)
10. **Don't reveal which answer they prefer.** A trained interviewer will probe both directions ("why not the opposite?") without telegraphing. [Source: Road to Offer pushback](https://www.roadtooffer.com/blog/case-interview-pushback-challenge)
11. **Don't rescue a struggling candidate too quickly.** Letting silence sit is part of the test — composure under pause. [Source: Interview IQ poker face](https://interviewiq.com.au/how-do-you-deal-with-poker-faced-interviewers)
12. **Don't ask leading questions.** "Don't you think the cost side is the issue?" violates training; better is "what would you investigate next?" [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
13. **Don't grade based on getting the "right" number.** Process and structure are what's scored, not the final estimate. [Source: IGotAnOffer market sizing](https://igotanoffer.com/blogs/mckinsey-case-interview-blog/market-sizing)
14. **Don't compare candidates verbally** ("the last person got this in 2 minutes"). Distorts the experience. [Source: Camden Kelly](https://camdenkelly.com/fired-before-youre-hired-five-ways-to-ruin-any-interview/)
15. **Don't multitask or check phone.** Sends a "you don't matter" signal and biases recall. [Source: Camden Kelly](https://camdenkelly.com/fired-before-youre-hired-five-ways-to-ruin-any-interview/)
16. **Don't cut the candidate off mid-thought** unless they're clearly off-track and time is critical. [Source: PrepLounge case structure](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/case-interview-structure-20493)
17. **Don't ask trick questions with no purpose.** Every question should map to a competency on the rubric. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview behavioral](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/consulting-behavioral-fit-interview)
18. **Don't share your own opinion on the case.** "I think the answer is X" biases the candidate's response. [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
19. **Don't drift into a coffee chat in round 1.** Round-1 must rigorously test case skill — partner-round can be more conversational. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
20. **Don't penalize hesitation if the candidate ends up structured.** Pause-then-organize is a positive signal, not a negative one. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview structure](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)
21. **Don't grade halo-effect from the resume.** Trained interviewers re-evaluate on case performance, not pre-read. [Source: Casebasix interview pitfalls](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/consulting-interview-pitfalls-avoid-rejection)
22. **Don't make the candidate feel stupid.** MBB explicitly trains for psychological safety — interviewers want best performance, not embarrassed candidates. [Source: Camden Kelly](https://camdenkelly.com/fired-before-youre-hired-five-ways-to-ruin-any-interview/)
23. **Don't ramble or over-explain the prompt.** Crisp, controlled delivery models executive communication. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview structure](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)
24. **Don't repeat the prompt unprompted.** If the candidate didn't catch it, let them ask. [Source: PrepLounge clarifying](https://www.preplounge.com/consulting-forum/clarifying-questions-1786)
25. **Don't grade on personality match.** Bain/McKinsey/BCG explicitly call out unconscious bias risk in interviewer training. [Source: Casebasix interview pitfalls](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/consulting-interview-pitfalls-avoid-rejection)
26. **Don't interrupt PEI stories to skip to the impact.** The story arc itself is the signal. [Source: IGotAnOffer PEI](https://igotanoffer.com/blogs/mckinsey-case-interview-blog/mckinsey-pei-how-to-impress-your-interviewer)
27. **Don't accept first answer to PEI** — drill with "tell me more about your specific role." [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview PEI](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-pei-questions)
28. **Don't use the same case across the day's panel** — risks contamination if candidates compare notes. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
29. **Don't assume silence = stuck.** Strong candidates pause to think; pushing them to fill silence is a tell of bad facilitation. [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
30. **Don't push ideology** ("the only right way is McKinsey way"). The case should be objective-neutral. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview McKinsey](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/mckinsey-case-interview)
31. **Don't give partial credit for a bad framework "because the candidate tried."** Standards must be consistent. [Source: Casebasix interview pitfalls](https://www.casebasix.com/pages/consulting-interview-pitfalls-avoid-rejection)
32. **Don't conduct the case while reading the prompt for the first time.** Partners are expected to know the case cold. [Source: CaseCoach](https://casecoach.com/b/whats-different-between-a-first-round-and-final-round-interview-at-bain-bcg-or-mckinsey/)
33. **Don't disclose the case "answer" at the end** — protects integrity of the case for future candidates. [Source: Caseinterview.com frameworks](https://caseinterview.com/case_interview_frameworks.pdf)
34. **Don't use jargon the candidate likely doesn't know** without defining it. Tests communication, not insider vocabulary. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview structure](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-structure)
35. **Don't talk more than the candidate.** Rule of thumb: the candidate should speak ~70-80% of the time during a case. [Source: HackingTheCaseInterview](https://www.hackingthecaseinterview.com/pages/case-interview-guide)
